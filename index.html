About Me

I am a Ph.D Candidate and (past) NSF GRFP fellow at the University of Notre Dame in the Department of Physics and Astronomy. My research focuses on the direct imaging of exoplanets and cold exoplanet demographics.

I received my M.S. degree from Notre Dame in January 2022. I got a B.S. in Physics and Mathematics from Missouri State University in May 2017.

In my free time, I love skiing, cross-stitching, and the outdoors.

I plan on graduating in Spring 2023 and am on the job market this Fall!

Research

  • Exoplanet Demographics Simulations

    Developing realistic planet population simulations for use in direct imaging yield calculations

  • Observations of Astrometric Accelerating Stars

    Combining astrometric accelerations, radial velocity measurements and direct imaging follow-up to detect planetary and brown dwarf companions as well as constrain masses and 3D orbits

  • Occurrence Rates of Cold Giant Planets

    Using long-term radial velocity trends and high-contrast imaging follow-up to calculate an occurrence rate of giant planetary companions to K stars
